  • star_half {% translate 'What is the "reliability" variable?' %}

    {% blocktranslate trimmed %} In a concern of transparency, the information provided by Mixtures.info is accompanied by a reliability index, which indicates the level of certainty of the delivered information. {% endblocktranslate %}

    • {% translate 'Hypothetical:' %} {% blocktranslate trimmed %} the sources are not sufficient to reach a conclusion, because they are rare or contradictory. It is necessary to make hypotheses from the properties of the substances or from a few testimonies. {% endblocktranslate %}
    • {% translate 'Inferred:' %} {% blocktranslate trimmed %} the data is based on a large number of testimonies. Some scientific studies can support them. {% endblocktranslate %}
    • {% translate 'Proven:' %} {% blocktranslate trimmed %} the data are the result of a solid convergence of testimonies and scientific knowledge. {% endblocktranslate %}
  • vertical_split {% translate 'Why separate Synergy and Risk?' %}
    • {% blocktranslate trimmed %} The Synergy section compiles information about the effects of a combination and the mechanisms behind it, without assessing its dangerousness. It addresses a goal of accessibility of knowledge. {% endblocktranslate %}
    • {% blocktranslate trimmed %} The Risk section is for people who would like to know more concretely what is involved in mixing two substances. It addresses a goal of harm reduction. {% endblocktranslate %}

    {% blocktranslate trimmed %} Separating these two sections allows us to be more precise, to integrate more information, and to better address the needs of the readers: knowledge, safety, or both. People who want to integrate our data into third-party applications can choose the ones they are interested in. {% endblocktranslate %}

playlist_add_check {% translate 'Data quality and reliability' %}

{% blocktranslate trimmed %} The quality of the data provided by Mixtures.info is a subject we are particularly concerned of. {% endblocktranslate %}

{% translate 'https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:No_original_research' as or_url %} {% echosafe 'href="' or_url '"' as or_attrs %} {% blocktranslate trimmed %} Each piece of information must be sourced, i.e. accompanied by a link to a resource testifying the validity of the information. Mixtures.info is ideally not a place to produce original research, but a place to gather verifiable information. Note that while wikis or forums are valuable tools, often linked to many high-quality sources, they are not in themselves sufficient sources. {% endblocktranslate %}

{% blocktranslate trimmed %} Moreover, all information must be moderated in accordance with the quality of its origin. It is thus appropriate to distinguish: {% endblocktranslate %}

    {% blocktranslate trimmed %}
  • Sources of low to average reliability, such as testimonials or cross-checking of testimonies;
  • Sources of reasonable reliability, such as valid scientific studies cross-checked by testimonies;
  • Highly reliable sources, such as valid meta-studies cross-referenced with testimonials.
    • {% endblocktranslate %}

{% translate 'Example of an invalid contribution:' %}

{% blocktranslate trimmed %} Polyuse of stimulants and depressants leads to more abuse than with each product alone. There is a high risk of addiction. {% endblocktranslate %}
{% translate 'Example of a valid contribution:' %}
{% echosafe 'target="_blank" href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/9088780"' as r1_attrs %} {% echosafe 'target="_blank" href="https://erowid.org/experiences/exp.php?ID=4390"' as r2_attrs %} {% blocktranslate trimmed %} This study shows that polyuse of stimulants and depressants would correlate with more risky practices and side effects than stimulant use alone. For example, in this testimony, the author's addiction to alprazolam was a consequence of their weekly MDMA use. {% endblocktranslate %}
{% blocktranslate trimmed %} In the valid contribution, each piece of information is linked to its source: here, a study and a testimony. The study is probably a more solid source than the testimony, but it is not always possible to find one: the important thing is to be explicit about the nature of the source ("this study", "this testimony"), to not make information seem more reliable than it is. Also, since a single study is not very strong evidence, a conditional tone is used ("would be correlated"). {% endblocktranslate %}

{% blocktranslate trimmed %} In the end, the same thing was said as in the first example, but without asserting anything on its own and giving an accurate picture of the state of knowledge. These differences may seem minor, but applying these guidelines allows the reader to verify the information. False data can be life threatening, hence it is unthinkable to ask to be taken at face value. {% endblocktranslate %}
